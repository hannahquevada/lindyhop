Lindy Hop Moves

There are two main types of Lindy Hop steps: 6-count steps and 8-count steps. Lindy Hoppers can transition between both types of steps while dancing to the same song. Many Lindy Hoppers change the steps slightly to add their own flair to it. This is known as styling or variations. The following are some steps that Lindy Hoppers learn all over the world.

Swing Outs

This is an iconic 8-count step that is among the first steps that Lindy Hoppers learn. Eight-count steps follow the rhythm of 1, 2, 3-and-4, 5, 6, 7-and-8.

Frankie Sixes

This is a well-known sequence of multiple 6-count steps created by Frankie Manning himself. Six count steps follow the rhythm of 1, 2, 3-and-4, 5-and-6. The following video shows what the sequence looks like.

Aerials

Aerials is an advanced sub-type of steps where one dancerâ€™s (usually the follower's) two feet leave the floor. These steps are more flashy and stunt-like, which is why they are usually reserved for choreographed performances and more experienced dancers. Some steps include the Lindy Throw-out, the Treehouse and the Lindy Backflip.

A comprehensive list of aerials and tutorials for each of them can be found here, though variations and completely new aerials continue to be invented every day. Do note that Lindy Hoppers who have not done aerials before SHOULD NOT be doing this at home. It is safer to learn aerials under the guidance of a dance instructor and the presence of safety mats.
