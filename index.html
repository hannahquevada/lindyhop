What is Lindy Hop?

Play this for ambience while you read.

Lindy Hop is a partnered dance that originated from 1920s Harlem, USA. It involves a leader and follower dancing to jazz or swing music (like the song playing right now!). The dance itself broke barriers between black and white communities and has strong ties to African American social dancing culture.
Lindy Hop is named after Charles Lindbergh, an aviator who made a groundbreaking flight across the Atlantic Ocean in 1927. To commemorate his accomplishment, many songs, business and recipes etc. were named after him, including Lindy Hop.

Lindy Hop Back Then

Lindy Hop was first danced in 1928 at the Savoy Ballroom in Harlem, New York. Shorty ‘George’ Snowden is credited with inventing and naming the dance during a long dance marathon held in the ballroom. As one of the few ballrooms that allowed the White and Black communities to dance together, people from both communities were able to share dance moves and inspire each other. Thus, Lindy Hop became more refined and flourished.

Lindy Hop was a popular dance throughout the late 1920s to 1940s.

Whitey’s Lindy Hoppers was the first dance troupe to popularise Lindy Hop. Its members were some of the best dancers from the Savoy ballroom. The talented troupe performed in several countries and movies. One of their most well-known performances can be found in the video below.

Whitey’s Lindy Hoppers performing in the 1941 film Hellzapoppin.

The dance troupe disbanded in 1942 as the males were enlisted into the army during World War II. Other partnered dances and music genres became more popular afterwards.

Frankie Manning & Norma Miller.

In the 1980s, two members from the Whiteys’ Lindy Hoppers Troupe, Norma Miller and Frankie Manning started teaching the dance to subsequent generations of dancers. Not only did they revive the dance in America, but they also taught it to dancers from other countries, inspiring the next wave of Lindy Hoppers all over the world.

Lindy Hop Today

Today, Lindy Hop continues to unite people from all over the world. It is performed across several countries in workshops, competitions and social dancing settings. L

The 2019 International Lindy Hop Championships partnered up dancers from Sweden, Korea, America, Brazil, Lithuania and the UK.
